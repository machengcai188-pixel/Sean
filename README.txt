World Cup Predictor

Open the tool:
1. Double-click "Open World Cup Predictor.command".
2. Click "Refresh FIFA data" inside the app.
3. Use "Show matches for" to view only one country's currently published fixtures, or leave it on "All teams".
4. Choose an official FIFA fixture or pick two teams manually.

Future fixtures:
- If a knockout-stage opponent has not been determined, the match will not appear under a country yet.
- Refresh FIFA data after the group stage. Newly published matches for the selected country will automatically appear.

What comes from FIFA:
- FIFA World Cup 2026 fixture list, stadiums, match dates, scores and groups.
- Team rosters, player position, jersey number, age, height, weight, caps and goals when available.
- Country abbreviations such as COL = Colombia and UZB = Uzbekistan.

Time display:
- Match times are shown in America/New_York time with EDT/EST shown beside the time.

What the model estimates:
- Technical ability, tactical intelligence, physical profile, production, consistency, big-game profile, defensive contribution, leadership and modern analytics.
- Some of these are not directly published by FIFA in the free official endpoint, so they are editable assumptions in the Player Assumptions panel.

How the prediction works:
- Each player receives a weighted overall score.
- Elite players receive an additional star score from international caps, career goals and World Cup production. A superstar can therefore move the team prediction more strongly.
- The team score uses likely best XI, squad depth, positional balance, current tournament form and match context.
- Official FIFA match data from the previous eight World Cups creates a tournament-pedigree score. This separates established World Cup powers from teams with little tournament history.
- Completed matches are backtested against their real results. The tool then adjusts draw probability and score-gap confidence when the original model has been too decisive or too cautious.
- The output is A win, B win or tie with estimated probabilities.

Important:
This is a decision-support tool, not a guarantee. Soccer has red cards, injuries, tactics, travel, weather and plain randomness.
